About me

I am a prospective Mechanical Engineering undergraduate at the University of Maryland, College Park, minoring in Nuclear Engineering (Expected GPA: 4.0). My journey began in Skopje, Macedonia, instilling in me a drive for perfection and resilience.


To me, engineering is more than just math and physics; it is the fundamental process of iterating through critical problems to find the most robust solutions. Through my academic journey and various projects, I've developed a strong foundation in and interest in Reliability and Systems Safety, encompassing mathematical modelling and statstical optimization. This led me to my interest in the field of Probabilistic Risk Assessment, and Fitness-for-Service assessments (FFS), blending the field of preditcive computing and physical engineering.

Life Outside Academics

Outside the engineering world, I find joy and fulfillment in photography — capturing landscapes, street scenes, and candid moments wherever I travel. I definetely am by no means professional, but it is a nice creative outlet from the academic rigor.


I'm an avid skier who lives for the adrenaline of fresh powder days, a dedicated runner always chasing new personal records, and a passionate traveler with an insatiable curiosity for new cultures, cuisines, and landscapes. These pursuits keep me balanced, energized, and inspired.

However, this all stemmed from my love for listening to music and appreciating the artistry behind it. I try to listen to a wide variety of genres, and explore how music evolves through time and culture, while always enjoying the roots of what made the music what it is for me.
